# Pathfinder — Garden Crew

## Purpose & audience

A small language for volunteer crews running a shared community garden. A reader — typically a new plot coordinator — should be able to use it to set up a garden crew that keeps producing after the founding enthusiasts move on.

## Scope

In: how the crew organises itself — agreements, stewardship, and recurring care. Out: horticulture itself (what to plant, soil chemistry), land tenure and council negotiations, and fundraising. Those tensions are real but belong to other bodies of knowledge.

## The gradient

Organisational scope, descending: **Whole Garden** (agreements binding everyone, band 1–9) → **Beds** (responsibility for a patch, band 10–19) → **Practices** (recurring acts of care, band 20–29). A pattern sits at the scale of the group it binds.

## Landscape & key references

- *A Pattern Language* (Alexander et al., 1977) — form and gradient conventions; patterns 170–178 cover adjacent ground (gardens as places, not crews).
- Group Works deck (groupworksdeck.org) — group-process patterns that complement the Practices band.
- Ostrom, *Governing the Commons* (1990) — the evidence base behind Shared Plot Charter.

## Evidence & provenance

Mined July 2026 from the maintainer's notes on three community gardens (two thriving, one folded) and the commons-governance literature. Bed Stewards carries three independent known uses; the rest of the language is younger. Confidence is rated accordingly.

## Conventions

Standard skill conventions throughout (confidence 0/✻/✻✻, rule of three). Bands leave gaps for growth.

## Growth queue

- *Tool Library* — shared tools rust or vanish, but personal tools exclude newcomers (Practices).
- *Season Opening* — a year needs a visible start, but rituals feel forced when imposed (Whole Garden).
