# 10. Bed Stewards ✻✻

…once a [Shared Plot Charter (1)](001-shared-plot-charter.md) says what is
shared, the beds themselves still need someone to answer for them…

## Problem

**What belongs to everyone is tended by no one, but assigning beds as private
property makes members strangers to the rest of the garden.**

The tragedy-of-the-commons failure mode and the allotment failure mode are
both real, and they are opposites. Gardens that made everything communal
watched the unglamorous beds go feral by midsummer. Gardens that fenced
everything into private allotments watched members stop talking to each
other. The resolution used by every thriving crew we found is stewardship:
named responsibility without exclusive ownership.

## Therefore

**Give every bed a named steward who answers for its condition but does not
own its produce or its use. Rotate stewardship yearly, and make the handover
a conversation, not a list.**

## Known uses

- Northcote community garden: steward tags on every bed since 2019; the only
  garden of the three studied with zero abandoned beds.
- The folded garden adopted stewards in its final year — too late, but the
  stewarded beds were the last ones alive.
- Open-source maintainership (CODEOWNERS files) is the same resolution to the
  same tension in a different commons.

## Cautions

Stewardship drifts toward ownership if rotation is skipped — the second year
a steward keeps "their" bed, produce-sharing quietly stops.
