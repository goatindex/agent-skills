# 20. Weekly Water Roster ✻

…[Bed Stewards (10)](010-bed-stewards.md) answer for their beds, but no
steward is at the garden every day, and plants do not wait…

## Problem

**Watering must happen more often than any one member visits, but ad-hoc
"someone will get it" coverage fails precisely in the weeks — heatwaves,
holidays — when it matters most.**

Both thriving gardens in the evidence base independently converged on the
same artifact: a paper grid by the tap, names against days. The interesting
part is what did not work first — group-chat coordination, which produced
diffusion of responsibility exactly as the bystander literature predicts.

## Therefore

**Put a physical roster at the water source — one name per day, claimed a
week ahead. A blank day visible to everyone is the alarm; a full grid is the
all-clear.**
