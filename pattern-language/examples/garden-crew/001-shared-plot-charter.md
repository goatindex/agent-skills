# 1. Shared Plot Charter ✻

…this is the largest pattern in the language; everything below it assumes the
crew has settled what "shared" means before anyone claims a bed…

## Problem

**A garden only thrives when people feel free to act on it, but unwritten
assumptions about who may pick, prune, or replant curdle into resentment the
first time two members' assumptions collide.**

Every folded community garden we looked at died the same way: not from pests
or drought but from an unresolved dispute that had no agreed ground to stand
on. Commons research (Ostrom) says the same — durable commons have explicit,
locally-made rules, however short. The failed garden in our evidence base had
none; the two thriving ones each had a page.

## Therefore

**Before the first season, write a one-page charter together: what is shared,
what is personal, how a newcomer joins, and how a dispute gets settled. Post
it where people actually stand, and re-ratify it — briefly — every year.**
